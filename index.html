What is Yoga?

Believe it or not, yoga originated thousands of years ago (approx.1000-500 BC).. India is the first known country to discover the effects yoga has on the mind and body.

To be exact, the translation of yoga is to yoke, join". Some interpretations would also say "discipline". However, all of the translations modern, or otherwise correlate to one meaning "union of the body and mind.

You do not have to practice poses for this concept. You can practice yoga through awareness of the relationship between your mind and body. Think about it, your body reacts to your mental state, vice-versa. Subconsciously many people change their physical state to alter their mental state (i.e running, walking, dancing), this is yoga. It's more than just a set of poses. It's a way to unite mind and body during every instance in our daily lives.

Make It a Commitment

It might be challenging in the beginning, but yoga is best used as as a committment. Try to discipline yourself. Here are some helpful tips to priorizing your yoga practice:

  • Place your mat somewhere visible for you to see as a gentle reminder to meditate, or begin your practice.
  • Treat your practice as an appointm,ent. Add it into your calendar.
  • If you are like many of us, it might prove more effective to sign up for a class at a local yoga studio to get a routine of atleast 2 sessions per week started.
  • Set an alarm on your phone with a nice little reminder note added to it.
  • Set a minimum goal to practice once a week in the beginning, you might find yourself gradually allowing yourself more time as you go.
  • Find a nice mantra or quote to meditate on as you go through with your daily life.

Special Conditions

There are some instances you may want to take precaution. Not all postures are for everybody. NO worries, you can find your own niche soon enough.

Take Special Care if.....

..you are pregnant.

Avoid lying on your belly for backbends. Try to stick more to seated twists or basic stretches. Consult with your physician to discuss the severity of your pregnancy before beginning asana practices. Also, check out the meditation techniques we've included.

..you are injured.

Consult with your doctor before beginning asana practices after an injury. Listen to your body. "NO PAIN, NO GAIN", is an empty adage in yoga. If something hurts asana practices could be detrimental to healing. Use props to guide your form, it's not about the endurance, it's about form.
